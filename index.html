Fascinatingly, Venus graces the night sky with its brilliance, earning the titles 'Evening Star' or 'Morning Star.' As the second planet from the Sun, Venus holds the distinction of being the hottest in our solar system, with temperatures reaching a scorching 465C- surpassing even Mercury, despite its closer proximity to the Sun. The planet's terrain consists of rocky surfaces and large expansive craters.

Jupiter, often referred to as the 'Gas Giant' because it lacks a solid surface, holds the title of being the largest planet in our solar system. Its colossal size is evident when considering that approximately 1,300 Earths could fit inside it. Due to its powerful magnetic field, it has over 75 moons with the largest one being Ganymede.

Mars, famously referred to as the Red Planet, occupies the fourth position in our solar system, and it is half in size in comparison to Earth. The planet is known for its surface feature composed of deserts and polar ice caps. Mars shares certain features with Earth, including mountains and weather conditions that include phenomena like tornados. Science continues to unravel the planets mysteries in hopes that one day this may also be known as Home.

Neptune is one of the coldest planets in the solar system. The temperature can plummet to -210C. This planet Is also known as an ice giant due to its atmospheric conditions. It experiences strong winds which reach speeds of over 2,400kmh.

Uranus, recognised as the ice giant, holds a collection of 27 moons, with the notable Titania and Oberon being the largest among them. Like Saturn, Uranus possesses a system of rings—13 in total, though their faintness makes them difficult to observe, hindering astronomers from capturing detailed images of these celestial features.
