Tourism in Covid-19 and Brexit

Brexit

The Brexit Countdown Officially Started. It Took Three Years For It To Be Fully Established.

Brexit has had mixed effects on tourism in Manchester. On one hand, it has led to fluctuations in visitor numbers and spending due to changes in travel regulations, currency fluctuations, and uncertainty surrounding the UK's relationship with the EU.

The Depreciation Of The Pound & Travel Restrictions

The depreciation of the pound following the Brexit referendum initially made the UK a more affordable destination for international tourists, potentially boosting visitor numbers. However, concerns over travel restrictions, visa requirements, and the impact of Brexit on the overall tourism experience may have deterred some travelers from visiting Manchester and the UK.​

The Labour Market & EU workers

Restrictions on freedom of movement and changes to immigration policies have led to challenges in recruiting skilled staff, potentially affecting the quality of service and visitor experience in Manchester's tourism industry.​

Covid-19

In the Covid-19 pandemic the streets use to be very quiet and it seems like there is no existence in this city. Everything was so quite; people were hoping to live a back to normal life after the pandemic. 

On the other hand, during pandemic the Brexit had started taken place. Therefore, things were massively changed since the pandemic ended. 

The research shows that Manchester's region welcomed nearly 12.5 million domestic tourists in 2019.  The number of business tourists had increased 50% in the last 10 years. This shows that there would be a 50% increase in employment.  The hotels were performing great, and they were the ones making most of the profit. 

Many countries were choosing venues across the Greater Manchester for their festivals, sports and leisure activities such as conferences, football and cricket matches, concerts, etc. The huge venues were also making most of the money which was also a key element of thrilling more options of employment for residents. 

In 2022, thing were getting better as people started visiting local places again and life was coming to existence once again.  Number of visitors were increasing massively throughout the year. After pandemic this was the time when domestic and inbound tourism was on peak.   

Manchester is well-known by its arts and culture, museums and galleries, food and drink, nightlife, music science, sports, shopping, hotels and Christmas markets.  From 1990 to 2019, this city had an increase in number of passengers travelling from Manchester airport.
